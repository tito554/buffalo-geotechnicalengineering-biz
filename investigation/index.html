In Buffalo, site investigation confronts the complex glacial geology of the Erie Basin, where lacustrine clays, dense till, and occasional soft organics govern foundation design. Our category addresses every phase of subsurface exploration in accordance with the New York State Building Code Chapter 18, ensuring compliance with IBC standards and local Buffalo permit requirements. Core methods include drilling, sampling, and In-Situ to map bearing strata and groundwater. The Standard Penetration Test (SPT) remains the backbone for preliminary soil classification, delivering N‑values that directly inform shallow footing capacity and liquefaction screening in the region’s saturated silts.

These investigations support deep excavations for downtown parking structures, bridge abutments along the Niagara Thruway, and industrial redevelopments on the city’s east side. For cohesive soils, our SPT program integrates seamlessly with laboratory triaxial testing and consolidation analysis, providing the complete strength and settlement profile required by NYSDOT and Buffalo DPW. By pairing field data with targeted lab work, we deliver geotechnical reports that de‑risk foundation design from bid stage through construction.

In Buffalo, a geotechnical investigation is the essential first step for any construction project, designed to characterize subsurface conditions and mitigate risks associated with the region's complex glacial geology. The area is underlain by lacustrine clays, glacial till, and occasional soft soils from Lake Erie’s historical lakebed deposits, all of which demand a rigorous understanding before design. Our approach fully complies with the New York State Building Code and local City of Buffalo ordinances, ensuring your project meets all regulatory benchmarks for safety. This comprehensive service integrates field testing, such as the Standard Penetration Test (SPT), with advanced geophysical methods, and is often paired with a detailed soil mechanics study to define engineering parameters.

Our investigation methodology is anchored in nationally recognized standards from ASTM International and AASHTO, ensuring data of the highest integrity. Fieldwork typically begins with exploratory borings where the SPT is executed at regular intervals to measure soil density and collect disturbed samples, strictly per ASTM D1586. For sensitive Buffalo clays, we retrieve undisturbed samples using Shelby tubes for strength testing back in our laboratory. When depth or access is challenging, we deploy geophysical techniques like seismic tomography to create continuous profiles of the subsurface, identifying bedrock depth and anomalies without the limitations of discrete borings.

Geotechnical investigations in Buffalo are critical for a spectrum of local projects, from revitalizing historic structures in the downtown core to new infrastructure along the I-190 corridor. For deep foundation design in the city’s compressible soils, the data we gather is indispensable for calculating skin friction and end bearing. On road and airport pavement projects, understanding the subgrade’s resilient modulus is key, often requiring a laboratory CBR test to be performed on representative soil samples. Complex projects near the Buffalo River or involving heavy structures frequently necessitate advanced strength testing, where we determine critical parameters like cohesion and friction angle through a triaxial test under consolidated-undrained conditions.

From initial site reconnaissance to the final geotechnical report, our process is transparent and deliverables-focused. Upon completing the field program, our laboratory testing suite provides the engineering data needed for design, with results synthesized into a clear, actionable report. This document includes borehole logs, laboratory data, and geophysical profiles, delivering definitive design recommendations for foundations, earth retention, and pavement sections. The ultimate value is risk reduction: we provide a precise subsurface model that eliminates guesswork, preventing costly change orders and ensuring your Buffalo project is built on a foundation of certainty.
